Reimagining Public Relations: Business Wire's Strategic Transformation

The Changing Landscape

The Kei team began by assessing the current state of the communications industry. "The media world is evolving rapidly," noted Kevin Farnham, one of Kei's partners. "Much of the content people consume now exists outside the traditional print and broadcast structure." This shift has created a complex web of challenges for communications professionals, from the convergence of PR and marketing departments to the rise of influencer marketing and direct brand-journalist relationships on social media platforms.

The challenge was clear: Business Wire needed to do more than just keep pace—it needed to redefine the industry itself.

A Human-Centered Approach

To tackle this challenge, the Kei team initiated extensive research, engaging a diverse array of stakeholders including communications teams, brand managers, journalists, PR agencies, and influencers. This human-centered approach revealed a clear need: an integrated platform that could support the entire communications workflow, from managing media contacts to creating content and measuring success.

The research uncovered several key themes:

  • Democratization of Media: Brands now have more direct channels to connect with end-users.
  • Content Arbitrage: There's a growing need to fine-tune outreach efforts to connect the right content with the right publishers.
  • Mobile-First Mentality: Content must be designed with a mobile-first approach.
  • Noise and Filtering: There's an opportunity to match content creators with journalists based on shared interests.
  • Building Stronger Connections: Brands and PR professionals want to build strong relationships with journalists without overwhelming them.

Designing the Future

Armed with these insights, the Kei team set out to design a revolutionary platform. The result was a concept for a seamless, AI-enhanced system that bridges the gap between strategy and execution, offering a truly integrated approach to communications.

The platform's proposed features include:

  • A Discovery phase using AI to identify messaging needs and trends
  • A Content Creation module with collaborative editing and AI-driven suggestions
  • A Relationship Management system bringing together all stakeholders
  • An intelligent Message Distribution system
  • A comprehensive Measurement & Refinement toolset

The platform was designed to adapt to each user's role, providing customized workflows that link seamlessly with other team members' tasks.

Market Validation

To ensure the concept resonated with potential users, an independent market research firm was commissioned to test it. The results were highly positive across all target segments:

  • 84% of corporate enterprise professionals expressed interest
  • 91% of PR agency professionals were interested
  • 81% of content influencers showed interest

The uniqueness of the concept was also well-received, with 78% of corporate enterprise professionals, 82% of PR agency professionals, and 65% of content influencers rating it as unique compared to other known services.

Pricing Strategy

The market research also helped determine the right pricing strategy. Using a price sensitivity analysis, the study found that:

  • Corporate enterprise professionals considered $3,000 per month as the ideal price point
  • PR agency professionals indicated a willingness to pay $3,500 per month
  • Content influencers, likely to use a more limited version of the platform, suggested an ideal price of $25-$30 per month

These figures not only validated the concept but also highlighted its potential to create a significant new revenue stream for Business Wire.

The Road Ahead

While the full implementation of this platform is still in progress, the project has already had a substantial impact on Business Wire's strategic direction. It has positioned the company at the forefront of the PR tech revolution, particularly in terms of AI integration.

The project highlighted several key lessons:

  • The power of systems thinking in solving complex, interconnected problems
  • The importance of designing for reality, considering all facets of implementation including hidden costs and organizational readiness
  • The value of bringing together diverse perspectives in the design process
  • The potential of AI to transform traditional industries when applied thoughtfully

As Business Wire continues to refine and develop this platform, it stands poised to potentially redefine the nature of public relations and corporate communications. The concept promises a future where messages find their intended audiences more effectively, relationships between brands and media are more mutually beneficial, and the impact of communication can be clearly measured and continually improved.

This transformation project serves as an example of how even established industries can be revitalized through innovative thinking and a commitment to solving real-world problems. It demonstrates the value of a strategic, human-centered approach to design and the potential for technology to address complex challenges in the communications landscape.

Research

We conducted research with a broad range of participants, including communications teams, brand managers, editors & journalists, PR agencies, investor relations teams, and influencers – each with their unique roles and responsibilities.

We explored their core needs and pain points, gathered perspectives on industry-wide trends, and gave opportunities for blue-sky thinking.

“My mailbox is flooded daily with hundreds of irrelevant pitches, most of which is trash.”

“I wish there was a way to really measure the quality of the coverage we are generating. We have very crude tools.”

Our research findings concluded that customers are first and foremost looking for:

  • An integrated offer that supports their workflow across managing media contacts, to creating content, to measuring success
  • An offering that makes less of a distinction between media roles, bringing journalists, content creators, social and influencers together as first class citizens in a communications ecosystem
  • Many customers don’t know if they are wasting their time by putting out an untargeted release or message to an overloaded journalists inbox.

What We Learned

Democratization

Brands no longer solely dictate the narrative. However, they now have the opportunity to connect with end-users directly, giving them more control over their stories.

Content Arbitrage

PR and brand professionals aim to fine-tune their outreach efforts to connect with the right press and publishers, while journalists and publishers seek to improve the fidelity of content matching.

Mobile First

With the growing prevalence of mobile devices, content must be designed with a mobile-first approach to accommodate the needs of influencers, journalists, and the public.

Additional Themes

Ideas that rose to the top during our research:

Noise

Amidst a sea of content, there's a great chance to match content owners with journalists and publishers based on shared interests, allowing for targeted and effective communication.

Tinder for PR

Journalists and publishers are overwhelmed with pitches, making it challenging to find the most relevant and valuable stories.

Building Stronger Connections

Brands and PR professionals want to build strong relationships with journalists without spamming them. They need to connect with those genuinely interested in their stories.

Better Filters

Journalists want more effective ways to discover pertinent information among the countless pitches they receive daily.

Social Transactions

It's crucial for brands and PR agencies to understand the nature of their relationships with editors and journalists, differentiating between intimate and transactional connections to foster successful collaborations.

News Bloggers

The rise of quasi-news bloggers has created confusion about how to maintain relationships with these influential figures in the media landscape.

SEO

Many teams overlook SEO, reducing the effectiveness of their campaigns.

Eroded Trust

The erosion of trust between journalists and brands has created tension in the PR ecosystem, emphasizing the need to maintain healthy interpersonal relationships.

Transparency

As technology demands increase transparency, brands must develop the skills to operate comfortably in this new environment.

Multimedia

Despite its importance, multimedia integration in PR is often an afterthought. Brands must also focus on controlling the final appearance of their press releases to end consumers.

Disintermediation

With virtually no barriers to information, distribution strategies need to adapt to this new reality.

Relationships are Regional

Cultural and regional differences influence the preferred methods of maintaining relationships, which brands and PR professionals need to be aware of.

Pay-Wall Bait

Editorial and investigative reporting publishers aim to find stories that encourage readers to move past pay-walls. Optimizing content to drive this behavior is essential.

Nimble

Brands should be more agile to capitalize on the real-time nature of most news and navigate multiple news cycles.

Influencers

Managing relationships with influencers, who follow different rules than traditional media figures, can be challenging. Brands need better tools and platforms for this purpose.

Personalized Pitches

Streamlining and automating the personalization process for pitches can help make content more relevant and appealing to specific journalists and their readers.

Narrowcast

The rise of social and self-publishing has created niche channels, challenging the traditional concept of broad distribution via wire services.

Measurement Challenges

PR professionals struggle to find accurate ways of measuring their campaigns' effectiveness and attributing traffic.

Plug & Play Content

Journalists need easily customizable content, including multimedia assets, to meet the demands of higher throughput and faster turnaround times.

Measurement

Although metrics are vital, many key variables remain poorly measured or captured, making it difficult to gauge the effectiveness of campaigns.

Strategy

After synthesizing user research, the competitive landscape, business objectives and financial considerations, we set to work on setting an overall strategy for the endeavor.

We believed that the solution to address the needs of a modern communications workflow was the creation of a two-sided media exchange that included:

  • Content Creation
  • Relationship Management
  • Message Distribution
  • Measurement & Refinement

The big question

How can we move from helping organizations with press releases to being the trusted partner for communications?

Initial Product Concept

Our initial concept was illustrated through user scenarios of four different key customer types:

  • PR Professional
  • Brand Manager
  • Account Director
  • Journalist

Screens shown are unsequential samples from these scenarios.

Content Spider Diagram

An engaging way to explore topical and related content. Size indicates real-time relevance and related content is illustrated through branching. Multiple viewing options are available in the tab structure above the diagram.

Create a Message: Article

Keyword suggestions illustrate SEO and other statistically successful terminology for use. Another system suggestion illustrates the availability of a white paper based on the subject matter.

The asset library at the bottom of the screen illustrates a company-managed asset library that can be used for messaging purposes. As the content and keywords change, key influencers are auto-populated.

Relationships Overview

A dashboard view of relationships. Under each ‘band’ is the ability to tip down the area to see more of any given grouping.

Entities’ most recent story or social activity is shown on primary card. Secondary information (found in the ‘three dots’ menu) reveal functionality such as contact history.

Publication Page

The ‘system version’ of a this page is an un-editable snapshot of publicly found information – compiled by the platform. For any type of entity (publications, companies, influencers, etc), visitors can toggle between system-controlled and entity-maintained versions (not shown).

Entity maintained pages can also contain available assets (media kit, logos, videos, releases, etc.)

Contact / Source Page

In this example, a journalist is looking for sources for an article. The Communications area includes a running log of previous communications with the contact.

The opportunity to instantly connect with the contact appears under her contact information. Setting an appointment, asking a question and emailing are the ways that this contact prefers to be contacted.

Co-Design

Following our Initial Concept work and executive team approval process, we conducted a round of co-design sessions with a subset of participants from our initial research. They were shown scenarios that best matched their roles and responsibilities. Users commented and modified these concepts freely, giving insight into how to best design to their needs.

Product Concept v2

Feedback gathered in co-designs and from internal teams set us up for the next round of Product Concept refinements – illustrated again through step-by-step user scenarios for key customer types.

The following examples are a small subset of the full scenario walkthrough.

Selecting the Audience

In this screen, the user selects the desired audience for their message. The system provides recommendations based on past behavior, frequent contacts, message content analysis, and opted-in journalists interested in receiving such information.

Message Scheduling

This screen allows the user to schedule the message easily, with the system providing smart suggestions on the best time to publish based on the organization's activities and global events to manage potential competing messages.

Message Performance Metrics

In this screen, the user can view real-time performance results of their message. The page updates with campaign results over time, providing immediate insights into effectiveness.

Agency Managing a Crisis

Problem Identification

The system analyzes the event's timing and helps the user identify the issue. It suggests possible response options, ranging from general actions like tweeting or releasing a statement to specific actions like issuing a particular tweet or retweeting someone.

Event Report Creation

The agency can generate a comprehensive report for their client after identifying the issue. The platform enables the user to include graphs, example tweets, and article mentions in the report while also providing a preview of the final document.

Report Distribution Management

This screen allows the user to manage lists and roles for report distribution. The user can also request approval from a senior colleague before sending the report.

Monitoring Metrics

Organizational Health and Performance Overview

In this screen, key stakeholders can view the organization's overall performance or delve into individual workspaces or specific messages. The main goal is to identify broad trends and correlations across the business that might not be evident to a project-focused individual.

Brand Manager & Influencer in a Two-sided Marketplace

Managing a Brief & Providing Brand Access

This screen offers a project brief, a deliverable timeline, and access to pre-existing brand materials, and additional content that the influencer may want to utilize to enhance their content.

Influencer-Specific Media Kit and Style Guides

Upon engaging an influencer, this screen displays a media package with access to detailed project information and relevant resources.
