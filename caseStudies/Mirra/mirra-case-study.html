Mirra

Shaping the Future of Spatial Computing

Introduction

In the fast-paced world of extended reality (XR), Kei embarked on a mission to revolutionize our digital inter-actions. The journey began with a bold idea: to create a platform that would make XR accessible to everyone. From sketching out initial concepts to overcoming tech-nical hurdles, the project evolved through a series of challenges and breakthroughs. Each step was a blend of planning and hands-on problem-solving, all aimed at building a user-friendly experience.

The Vision: Democratizing XR

From its inception, Mirra was more than just another tech project for Kei. It was a mission to democratize XR, making the creation of augmented and virtual reality experiences as accessible as building a website. As Kevin Farnham, a partner at Kei, explained in a recent interview:

We saw the enormous potential of spatial computing to revolutionize how humans interact with data and each other. Our goal was to ensure that everyone, regardless of their technical background, could participate in this revolution.

The Challenge: Simplifying Complexity

The XR landscape in 2016 was largely unexplored, with no established design patterns or interaction paradigms. Kei's team had to balance the need for simplicity with the power required for professional-grade XR creation. As one team member recalled:

"We were essentially creating a new language for spatial computing. Every interaction, every menu, every gesture had to be intuitive yet powerful enough to handle complex 3D designs."

This challenge exemplified Kei's strength in holistic problem-solving. The team didn't just focus on the user interface; they considered the entire ecosystem of XR creation, from the initial concept to the final experience across multiple platforms.

A Human-Centered Approach

Kei's human-centered approach was crucial in making Mirra accessible to a wide range of users. The team conducted extensive user testing with Mirra's initial customer base, using real-world feedback to refine the platform.

This iterative process led to key features like the platform-agnostic design, allowing creators to publish their work across multiple XR platforms without additional effort. As Tim Cook, CEO of Apple, noted about the potential of AR:

Building for the Future

While developing Mirra, Kei kept an eye on the future of spatial computing. The team designed the platform with extensibility in mind, including plans for AI-driven procedural environment creation. As a system architect on the project explained:

"We weren't just building for today's needs. We were laying the groundwork for the next generation of spatial computing tools."

This forward-thinking approach positioned Mirra as more than just a tool – it became a platform ready to evolve with emerging technologies.

Impact and Legacy

Mirra's impact was felt across industries, with adoption by major organizations like The World Bank, SFMOMA, Logitech, and NBC Universal. But beyond its commercial success, Mirra represented a paradigm shift in XR creation.

As noted in VR Scout:

What's most exciting about a development tool this easy to use is its potential to attract a whole new market previously intimidated by the complexity of the still-emerging technology.

By making AR and VR design accessible to non-technical users, Kei and Mirra opened up new possibilities for education, entertainment, business, commerce, and art.

Lessons for the Future

The Mirra project reinforced Kei's belief in the transformative power of user-centric design and systems thinking. It demonstrated that even the most advanced technologies can be made accessible with the right approach.

Looking ahead, the lessons from Mirra continue to shape Kei's approach to emerging technologies:

01.
Democratization of Technology

Making complex tools accessible drives innovation and creativity across industries.

02.
Systems-Level Design

Considering the entire ecosystem, from creation to consumption, is crucial for building truly transformative platforms.

03.
User-Centric Innovation

Real-world user feedback and iterative design are essential when creating tools for emerging technologies.

04.
Future-Proofing

Designing with extensibility and adaptability in mind ensures longevity in rapidly evolving tech landscapes.
