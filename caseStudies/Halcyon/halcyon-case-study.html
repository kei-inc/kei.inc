Halcyon

Reimagining Energy Intelligence:
The Birth of Halcyon

Introduction

In a world grappling with the complexities of energy transition, Halcyon emerged with a clear mission: to accelerate this transition by making business decisions faster, more accurate, and better informed. The company's AI-assisted search and information platform cuts through the noise, uncovers buried data, and quickly surfaces what matters most to professionals in the energy sector.

Nat Bullard
Halcyon
Chief Strategy Officer

The scale of the challenge is staggering. As Nat Bullard, Halcyon's Chief Strategy Officer, puts it: "Globally, we need just under $200 trillion invested in the next three decades to reach net-zero emissions by mid-century. In 2023, we invested $1.8 trillion. Deploying that money is a capital markets challenge, but it is also a technical, business strategy, planning, and regulatory challenge."

The numbers back this up. According to the International Energy Agency, global energy investment reached $2.4 trillion in 2022, with clean energy investments accounting for over $1.7 trillion. This massive market is exactly what Halcyon is poised to serve.

Bruce Falck
Halcyon
CEO

Inception Story

Halcyon was founded in April 2023 by Bruce Falck, former head of Twitter's ad team. His journey from building "incredible machines to serve ads" to serving the planet began with a desire to make a positive impact. A conversation with Andrew Beebe at Obvious Ventures, known for its "world positive investing," set Falck on the path that led to Halcyon's creation.

Initially exploring carbon trading markets, Falck's direction shifted after conducting over 100 customer interviews. He identified a critical need: organizations were struggling to navigate the energy transition efficiently.

Every single one of our customers is struggling to navigate the energy transition,"Falck explains. "They spend an inordinate amount of time understanding energy policy and regulation to move the world forward. We help them do that more efficiently.

This insight led to the development of a data platform aimed at professionals navigating the energy transition, a move that resonated strongly with potential clients in the climate and energy sectors.

Business Design & concept

The Challenge

The energy sector is laden with complexities. Professionals spend considerable time sifting through vast amounts of regulatory and market data to make informed decisions. Nat Bullard articulates the problem: "Today's information landscape is opaque, fragmented, noisy, and ever-expanding. That is a source of intense frustration for any analyst in a strategy or policy role. But with emerging artificial intelligence capabilities, it is also an opportunity."

The scale of this data challenge is mind-boggling. A study by IDC found that the volume of data worldwide is expected to grow to 175 zettabytes by 2025, with a significant portion related to energy and climate. This data explosion underscores the need for advanced processing tools like Halcyon's platform.

Falck emphasizes the potential impact: "If we can help someone get to a good answer in two hours versus two days or two weeks, that's going to have a huge impact. The way I think about it is we're removing sand from the gears."

Kei's involvement was instrumental in Halcyon's early development. Our team explored concepts, created prototype product sketches, defined potential value propositions, conducted market research and competitive analysis, and identified key pain points and opportunities for potential customers.

Reflecting on this phase, Falck notes;

It was company creation in the true sense.

Investment Deck & Funding

Securing investment was a critical early milestone for Halcyon. Kei worked closely with the team to shape the product vision and create a compelling investor narrative. We developed user personas, market analysis, competitive landscape overviews, and custom data visualizations.

This collaborative effort resulted in Halcyon securing $10 million in seed funding, providing the resources needed to turn the concept into reality. This funding success is particularly noteworthy given the challenges in the energy investment landscape. A survey by the World Economic Forum found that 40% of energy industry executives cite regulatory uncertainty as a major barrier to investment in clean energy. Halcyon's platform, designed to navigate these complex regulatory landscapes, clearly resonated with investors.

Company / Brand Strategy

A critically important aspect of our collaboration was developing a comprehensive brand strategy. Kei led a process that included interviews with company founders, stakeholders, and investors to understand the opportunities, shared beliefs, and commonalities.

"We kind of lacked ambition and imagination for what this thing could be," Falck admits. The work reshaped the team's perception of their mission and potential.

Our goal was to establish Halcyon as the trusted technology partner and leading information platform in the energy transition ecosystem while leaving latitude to potentially expand to additional market segments in the future. This strategy was crucial in positioning Halcyon to address a market that Bloomberg New Energy Finance projects will reach $2.1 trillion by 2030.

The resulting strategy included:

Brand Vision

The essential information platform for accelerating better decision making.

Brand Promise

Faster, more comprehensive access to the energy transition information that matters most.

Shared Belief

Technology can improve access to energy transition information, amplifying industry expertise to better keep pace with reality.

These strategic elements were carefully crafted to position Halcyon uniquely in the market, differentiating them from competitors and resonating with their target audience's needs and aspirations.

Product Strategy & Product Design

Artificial intelligence plays a central role in Halcyon's offerings. The company employs large language models (LLMs) and generative AI to process and interpret vast amounts of unstructured data.

Bullard explains in a blog post, "LLMs allow us to ask questions and get responses in human language. They make our work happen in a way that it could not before, allowing us to do something with software that would have been extremely expensive or impossible to do only with humans."

Halcyon goes beyond basic AI use, using retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) to ground LLMs in specific knowledge areas, ensuring accurate and contextually relevant responses. This technique is crucial for dealing with specialized areas such as regulatory policies and energy transition information.

Key features of Halcyon's platform include:

AI-Assisted Discovery

Purpose-built search infrastructure and know-ledge graph streamline research and analysis. An intuitive natural language interface makes it easy to interact with and interrogate data. Customizable filters, tags, operators, and outputs provide multiple pathways to relevant information.

Curated Data Catalog

Instantly accessible information aggregated from governments, utilities, corporations, and industry organizations. High-volume, rapid data ingestion from authoritative sources keeps users up-to-date. Transparent document library and query response citations include clickable sources to verify accuracy.

Collaboration Tools

Shareable and exportable workspaces foster collaboration and integrate into existing work-flows efficiently and securely.

The product design challenge was to create an interface that helps energy pro-fessionals move expediently through the system. Our goal was to create a multi-modal interface that felt familiar but provided UI for a completely new type of information retrieval system. We needed to provide industry experts with a high degree of information scan-ability and speed to answers.

We provided customer journey maps, feature concepts, interaction design models, UI design, low and high-fidelity prototypes, and a supporting design system.

Conclusion

Halcyon's journey from concept to execution offers a glimpse into the evolving landscape of AI-enhanced data technology. As sectors converge and professionals from diverse backgrounds pivot towards environmental solutions, Halcyon stands as a testament to the potential of cross-industry innovation.

The Halcyon project exemplifies Kei's model of engagement, where strategic design thinking can shape a company from its very inception, creating positive ripple effects that extend far beyond immediate project goals.

In the race against climate change, every efficiency gained can have outsized impacts. Halcyon's mission to make climate-related work more efficient and effective is a testament to the power of holistic, strategic design in shaping not just companies, but potentially the future of our planet.

Challenges remain in the complex and rapidly evolving energy sector, with regulatory landscapes shifting almost as quickly as the technology. Halcyon's success will depend not only on its technical prowess but also on its ability to navigate these choppy waters.

As Halcyon continues to grow, the foundation laid by Kei positions the company for long-term success. With a clear vision, strong brand, and powerful product offering, Halcyon is well-equipped to navigate the road ahead and make a significant impact in accelerating the energy transition.
