Producer

REMOTE - CONTRACT

Kei is a full-service creative studio that specializes in orchestrating complex projects. We bring together teams of specialists—strategists, designers, producers, writers—to launch and build brands with substance. We're looking for a Project Producer to be the operational backbone across our projects.

The position:

This is someone who keeps complex, multi-workstream projects moving. You'll work directly with Kei's founders and interface with clients and contractors to make sure nothing falls through the cracks—timelines stay on track, deliverables get delivered, and everyone knows what's happening and what's next.

We need someone organized, proactive, and comfortable working across multiple projects simultaneously. You'll run status meetings, maintain project timelines, chase deliverables, flag risks before they become problems, and generally be the person who makes sure things actually get done.

The offer:

  • Compensation: $6,500-8,000 per month (contractor rate)
  • Duration: 6 months with option to convert to full-time
  • Start: ASAP

What you will do:

Project Operations (50%)

  • Maintain and update project timelines and schedules across active projects
  • Track deliverables and deadlines, follow up with team members and contractors
  • Identify bottlenecks and risks early, propose solutions
  • Keep project documentation organized and accessible
  • Escalate scope, resourcing, or timeline risks with clear options and recommendations.

Communication & Coordination (35%)

  • Run weekly status meetings with internal team and clients
  • Prepare agendas, take notes, distribute action items
  • Serve as primary point of contact for project status questions
  • Ensure clients and team members have what they need to do their work

Process & Systems (15%)

  • Help build and refine Kei's project management processes as we grow
  • Establish templates and workflows that make future projects smoother
  • Contribute to improving how we work, not just managing current work

Who you are:

Required

  • 2-4 years project management experience, ideally at an agency, studio, or consultancy
  • Track record managing multiple projects simultaneously without dropping balls
  • Strong written and verbal communication—you can run a client meeting and write a clear status update
  • Highly organized with systems that work for you
  • Proactive—you flag issues and propose solutions, not just surface problems
  • Comfortable with ambiguity and building process as you go
  • Available for meetings across US time zones

Strongly Preferred

  • Experience in branding, design, or creative services
  • Familiarity with event production timelines and vendor coordination
  • Experience working directly with clients, not just internal teams

Bonus Points

  • Experience at a small studio or consultancy (under 20 people)
  • Background in hospitality, food & beverage, or lifestyle brands
  • You've helped build PM systems from scratch, not just inherited them

  • Resume or LinkedIn
