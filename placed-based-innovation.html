Place-Based Innovation Offer

Tailoring Solutions to Local Contexts

We're fascinated by how innovation can transform communities, cities, and regions. Place-based innovation is about developing solutions that are tailored to the unique characteristics, challenges, and opportunities of specific locations. It's an approach that's gaining traction globally, and for good reason.

Why Place-based Innovation Matters

Traditional, one-size-fits-all approaches to development often fall short because they don't account for local nuances. Place-based innovation, on the other hand, leverages local knowledge, resources, and networks to create more effective and sustainable solutions.

The impact of this approach can be significant. According to the European Commission's 2021 Regional Innovation Scoreboard, regions implementing place-based innovation strategies have experienced, on average, 2.3% higher annual GDP growth compared to regions without such strategies. While this might seem like a small number, in terms of economic growth, it's substantial and can translate to meaningful improvements in people's lives.

But here's the kicker: global investment in place-based innovation initiatives has grown from $12 billion in 2015 to $47 billion in 2022 - a nearly 300% increase, according to McKinsey & Company. If you're not already thinking about place-based innovation, you might be falling behind.

Ecosystem Orchestration

We help bring together diverse stakeholders to create systems-wide collaboration. This is increasingly important - and increasingly challenging. The United Nations' 2022 World Cities Report notes that the number of formal multi-stakeholder partnerships in urban governance has increased by 63% globally between 2015 and 2022.

These partnerships are crucial for addressing complex challenges that no single entity can solve alone. But let's be honest: dealing with multiple stakeholders, each with their own goals and communication styles, can be a real pain. We've got experience navigating these complex environments and helping everyone work towards common goals.

Experience Design and Service Innovation

We create location-specific experiences and services that resonate with both visitors and residents. This might involve designing public spaces that reflect local culture, developing digital platforms that enhance civic engagement, or creating tourism experiences that showcase a region's unique attributes.

Strategic Foresight and Scenario Planning

We assist organizations in navigating complex futures by developing robust strategies that account for multiple possible scenarios. This is particularly important in place-based innovation, where local conditions can change rapidly due to economic, social, or environmental factors.

Regenerative Business Model Innovation

We support the development of business models that create value for all stakeholders while regenerating natural and social capital. This approach helps places move beyond sustain-ability to become truly regen-erative, fostering long-term prosperity and resilience.

Impact Measurement and Learning Systems

We design and implement systems to measure impact and foster continuous learning. This ensures that place-based innovations contribute to both local prosperity and broader sustainability goals.

Practical Tools for Action

We've found that people love practical tools they can engage with. That's why we've developed checklists, readiness guides, and assessment tools that help communities and organizations understand where they are in their place-based innovation journey and what steps they need to take next.

Leveraging Technology for Community Engagement

We use digital tools for community engagement and data synthesis, allowing us to gather input from a wide range of stakeholders and turn that input into actionable insights.

The Untapped Potential of Rural Innovation

While much of the conversation around innovation focuses on urban centers, we've seen firsthand the potential for innovation in rural areas. There's often unexpected manufacturing capability and creative talent in these places. For young, ambitious people, rural areas can offer unique opportunities to make a significant impact.
