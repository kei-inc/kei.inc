We recognize that the most effective solutions to global challenges often emerge from local contexts. Our place-based innovation approach empowers communities, cities, and regions to create resilient, sustainable, and prosperous futures.

Approach

We apply systems thinking to place-based challenges, considering the entire ecosystem in which innovations operate. Our process includes:

01.
Holistic Assessment

We evaluate local assets, challenges, and opportunities within the context of global transitions.

02.
Collaborative Visioning

We facilitate the creation of shared visions that align local aspirations with broader sustainability goals.

03.
Strategic Prioritization

We help identify areas where local strengths can address global challenges, creating unique value.

04.
Innovative Implementation

We design and support the execution of place-based solutions that bridge local action and global impact.

05.
Adaptive Learning

We foster continuous learning and adaptation, ensuring strategies evolve with changing contexts.

Core Capabilities

01.
Ecosystem Orchestration

We bring together diverse stakeholders to create systems-wide infrastructure for collaboration, synergies, and alignment towards change. This approach can revitalize entire communities, from main street businesses to cultural institutions.

02.

We help design innovation processes that leverage local assets and global knowledge to drive sustainable transitions. Our approach goes beyond traditional economic development to create resilient economies that benefit both people and the environment.

03.

We assist organizations in navigating complex futures by developing robust strategies that account for multiple possible scenarios. This capability is crucial in helping places adapt to rapid changes in technology, climate, and society.

Our work in Humboldt, Kansas demonstrates the power of place-based innovation. By bringing together local entrepreneurs, city officials, and community leaders, we helped reimagine the town's future. Key outcomes included:

  • Creating a "Humboldt Vernacular" in architecture and design, promoting a unique sense of place and sustainable practices.
  • Establishing innovation hubs like makerspaces that build on local skills and traditions.
  • Developing strategies to attract remote workers and create new economic opportunities in sustainable industries.
  • Supporting businesses that contribute to community wellbeing and environmental restoration.
  • Implementing metrics that measure community vitality, cultural richness, and ecological health.

This comprehensive approach has transformed Humboldt's local economy and social fabric, creating a model for rural revitalization.
