Platform Narrative Project

Internal Developer Vignette

A conversation to gather insights and identify the right stories to tell

Context

What We're Doing

We're creating narrative vignettes that show how platform capabilities make Autodesk products and workflows better.


These aren't technical docs — they're stories told through real people doing real work that help others understand why platform matters.

Platform Framing

How We're Thinking About Platform

The platform enables products to do things together that they couldn't do alone.

Not Replacing — Amplifying

Revit, Fusion, Maya become more powerful because they connect to shared capabilities: identity, data, AI, collaboration. Infrastructure that no single product could build alone.

From Files to Flows

Moving from manual, file-based workflows to connected, AI-augmented experiences where context flows between tools, teams, and phases of work.

Framework

Three Tiers of Stories

Tier 1 — Clear & Achievable

18 months. Clear ownership, tangible value, real or nearly real.

Tier 2 — Interconnected

2-3 years. Multiple capabilities working together. This is where platform power becomes visible.

Tier 3 — Future-Facing

3-5 years. Inspirational vision. What becomes possible?

Internal Developer Story

The Basic Shape

A developer at Autodesk needs to build a new capability. In the past, this meant weeks of Slack messages, wiki diving, and documentation hunting. Now, AI-augmented tools get them from idea to working code dramatically faster. The capability they build then becomes available to customers through the Assistant.
Questions — Tier 1

The "Easy" Story

  • What's the most compelling before/after right now? What used to take weeks that now takes hours?
  • Which tools or workflows resonate most? CloudOS? Agentic code-gen? Compliance automation?
  • What's the cleanest example of something built fast that's now in customers' hands?
Questions — Tier 2

The "Complex" Story

  • How do developers learn about customer needs? Is there a feedback loop we could illustrate?
  • What happens when something crosses boundaries — data, AI/ML, and Assistant all at once?
  • Any examples where speed of development directly enabled a customer outcome?
Questions — Tier 3

The "Future" Story

  • What's on the horizon that changes how developers work in 2-3 years?
  • What would "developer superpowers" look like — things that feel almost science fiction today?
Questions — Story Shape

Getting the Story Right

  • Who's the right persona? Senior platform engineer? Newer developer? Industry team member?
  • What's the emotional hook? Speed? Creativity unlocked? Frustration eliminated?
Closing

What We're Looking For

Not exhaustive feature lists.
Not technical architecture.
Not every edge case.


We want the feeling of what's changed and what's coming — the story that makes someone say "I get why this matters."
