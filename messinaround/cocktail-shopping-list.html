Xmas Party Shopping & Tools

Everything you need for the Gold Rush & Hanmattan

Shopping List

Bourbon
Nothing fancy—Buffalo Trace, Evan Williams, Wild Turkey 101 all work great
1 bottle
Sweet Vermouth
Dolin Rouge or Cocchi Vermouth di Torino. Refrigerate after opening.
1 bottle
Angostura Bitters
The one with the oversized label. Accept no substitutes for this one.
1 bottle
Lemons
Fresh. One lemon yields about 1 oz juice. Squeeze same day.
2–3
Honey

small jar
Ice
You need it for shaking, stirring, and serving.

What You'll Make

Gold Rush — 2 oz bourbon, ¾ oz lemon, ¾ oz honey syrup. Shaken.
Hanmattan — 2 oz sweet vermouth, 1 oz bourbon, 2 dashes bitters. Stirred.

Tools

Shaker
A Boston shaker (metal tin + pint glass) or two-tin setup is ideal. Seal forms from temperature.
Home sub: A mason jar with lid works perfectly. Or a protein shaker bottle. Anything that seals and can take a beating.
Strainer
Hawthorne strainer with the spring coil. Sits on top of your shaker tin.
Home sub: If using a mason jar, the lid ring plus a tea strainer. Or just hold the ice back with a large spoon while pouring.
Jigger
For measuring. Japanese style (tall, with lines) or standard 1oz/2oz double jigger.
Home sub: A standard shot glass is 1.5 oz. Tablespoon measures work: 1 oz = 2 tbsp. Or just use a small measuring cup.
Mixing Glass
Heavy glass vessel for stirring. Holds cold well, gives you room to work.
Home sub: A pint glass. Or any large, sturdy glass. Even a big measuring cup works in a pinch.
Bar Spoon
Long twisted shaft for smooth stirring against the glass wall.
Home sub: A long thin spoon—chopstick even. Any long utensil that lets you stir smoothly without clinking.
Citrus Juicer
Handheld press or reamer for getting juice from lemons.
Home sub: Cut lemon in half, squeeze by hand over a small strainer to catch seeds. A fork jabbed into the flesh helps extract more juice.
Glassware
Rocks glass for the Gold Rush, coupe or cocktail glass for the Hanmattan.
Home sub: Any short tumbler for the Gold Rush. Any stemmed wine glass or small bowl-shaped glass for the Hanmattan. Use what you have.
