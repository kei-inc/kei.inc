Autodesk PSET Platform Narrative

Creating vignettes that tell the story of why platform matters

December 2025

The Client

Platform Services & Emerging Tech (PSET)

PSET is the platform organization within Autodesk — they build the shared capabilities that power products like Revit, Fusion, Maya, and AutoCAD.

They've created 8 capability strategy memos defining their 3-year vision. Now they need a way to tell that story to internal stakeholders, executives, and eventually customers.

The Problem

They Ran Out of Steam

The team started a narrative document last summer but it stalled. Too many cooks, too much abstraction, not enough tangible output.

The existing draft was too low-level (feature descriptions) without landing the "why should I care" for busy stakeholders.

They need something that can be used in presentations, shared with executives, and adapted for different audiences.

Strategic Frame

How We're Positioning Platform

Not Replacing Products — Amplifying Them

Revit, Fusion, Maya become more powerful because they connect to shared capabilities: identity, data, AI, collaboration.

From Files to Flows

Moving from manual, file-based workflows to connected, AI-augmented experiences where context flows between tools and teams.

⚠️ Politically sensitive: avoid language that makes product teams feel threatened or replaced.

The Deliverable

Modular Vignettes in PowerPoint

Not a video. Not an interactive microsite (for now). Modular slides that can be assembled, presented, and shared.

Each vignette tells a story through a persona facing a challenge, enabled by platform capabilities, achieving an outcome.

  • Scene-setting slides (the "philosophy" preamble)
  • Individual vignettes (modular, swappable)
  • Capability maps showing what platform services are involved
Story Structure

Four-Act Vignette Format

Act 1: Challenge

Persona faces a problem. "This is how it used to be. Remember how painful this was?"

Act 2: Solution Being Built

Platform capabilities enable something new. Developer story, infrastructure story.

Act 3: Resolution

Persona uses the solution. Days instead of weeks. Frustration eliminated.

Act 4: Reveal

Narrator explains what made it possible. "Here's what platform enabled."

Prioritization

Three Tiers of Vignettes

Tier 1 — Clear & Achievable (18 months)

Clear capability ownership, tangible value. Internal developer, Connected admin, External developer.

Tier 2 — Interconnected (2-3 years)

Multiple capabilities working together. Jordan's Air Fryer journey, budget alerts, developer response loop.

Tier 3 — Future-Facing (3-5 years)

Inspirational vision. AI-native design, foundation models, ecosystem at scale.

Example

Jordan's Air Fryer (Tier 2)

Jordan is an industrial designer tasked with creating a sustainable Air Fryer design in record time. Jordan is not a sustainability expert. In the past, this meant printing plans, mailing to consultants, waiting for redlines — weeks of delay.

Now, Jordan asks the Autodesk Assistant to validate the design against sustainability standards. The Assistant connects to real-time validation APIs (built by an internal developer using platform tools), shares scoped data with an external consultant, and Jordan completes the project in days instead of weeks.
